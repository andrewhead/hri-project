Instructions

In this activity, you will work with a computer algorithm to find a target image.

You will rank images based on their closeness to a target. The better you rank these images, the more likely you are to find the target image.

The algorithm will provide you new example images to rank.

To rank the examples:

To rank the images, you must drag them to sort them from best to worst. When you're happy with the ordering, click I Have Sorted the Images. The algorithm will fetch more examples for comparison.

To rank the images, look at the pair of images beneath the goal. Pick the one that you think is closest to the goal. Under that image, click on the button that reads This Image is Better. The algorithm will fetch more examples for comparison.

To complete the HIT:

The ranking task will automatically end after you give 20 rankings, or after you have found the target image. If the algorithm seems to stop providing helpful images, keep ranking until you have given 20 rankings. Then the task will end on its own.

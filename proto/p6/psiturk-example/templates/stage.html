Instructions

In this activity, you will guide a computer algorithm to find a target image. The computer will show you example images. Your will compare these examples. After you compare them, the computer will suggest new examples for comparison.

Each new set of examples from the computer will get you closer to the target image, based on your comparisons. Your job is to give accurate comparison judgments so that the computer rapidly finds the right target image.

To rank the examples, do the following.

There will be a row of examples on the page. Drag the examples to order them from best to worst.

When you're happy with the ordering, click I Have Sorted the Images. The algorithm will fetch more examples for comparison.

Eventually, the algorithm will give you an image identical to the target. When this happens, mark this image as the best. Then click Goal Achieved.

In some cases, after many comparisons, the algorithm may not reach the goal. If the algorithm seems to "get stuck", click on the Algorithm Got Stuck button to end the task. This button will only appear after 10 comparisons. You will still get compensated for this HIT if you click this button.

Goal

Sort images by how closely they resemble the goal image.
